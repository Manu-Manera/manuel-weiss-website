Scientific Background

The RAISEC model is based on over 50 years of research and millions of participants

1959

Foundational Research

John L. Holland begins his research on career choice and personality types at Johns Hopkins University.

1973

First Publication

Publication of the "Self-Directed Search" (SDS) - the first standardized test for career orientation.

1985

International Recognition

The model is validated and translated in over 30 countries. Over 1 million people have taken the test.

2000

Digital Revolution

Online versions and digital assessments are developed. Integration into modern HR systems.

2024

The Six Personality Types in Detail

A detailed analysis of the RAISEC dimensions

R - Realistic

Practical, hands-on, technical

Core Personality

Realistic people are practical, enjoy working with their hands, and prefer concrete, measurable results. They value honesty, directness, and practical problem-solving.

Strengths

  • Manual skills
  • Technical understanding
  • Problem-solving competence
  • Practical implementation

Modern Applications

In digital transformation, realistic types are particularly in demand for technology implementation, IoT development, and practical AI applications.

A - Investigative

Research-oriented, scientific, intellectual

Core Personality

Investigative people are curious, logical, and scientifically oriented. They love solving complex problems and gaining new insights.

Strengths

  • Analytical thinking
  • Research skills
  • Critical thinking
  • Scientific methodology

Modern Applications

Perfect for data science, AI research, quantum computing, and scientific innovations in the modern workplace.

I - Artistic

Creative, expressive, unconventional

Core Personality

Artistic people are creative, expressive, and value individuality. They prefer unconventional solutions and artistic forms of expression.

Strengths

  • Creativity
  • Innovation
  • Design thinking
  • Aesthetic sense

Modern Applications

Essential for UX/UI design, content creation, digital art, and creative technologies in the modern economy.

S - Social

Helpful, communicative, cooperative

Core Personality

Social people are helpful, empathetic, and enjoy working with others. They value interpersonal relationships and shared goals.

Strengths

  • Empathy
  • Communication skills
  • Teamwork
  • Emotional intelligence

Modern Applications

Critical for remote team leadership, digital collaboration, virtual counseling, and modern HR practices.

E - Enterprising

Leadership-oriented, sales-focused, ambitious

Core Personality

Enterprising people are ambitious, leadership-oriented, and sales-focused. They enjoy taking responsibility and driving innovation.

Strengths

  • Leadership qualities
  • Sales skills
  • Strategic thinking

Modern Applications

Indispensable for startup founding, digital transformation, e-commerce, and modern business models.

C - Conventional

Structured, organized, detail-oriented

Core Personality

Conventional people are structured, organized, and enjoy working according to fixed rules. They value order, precision, and systematic work methods.

Strengths

  • Organizational talent
  • Precision
  • Systematic work
  • Attention to detail

Modern Applications

Essential for process optimization, compliance, data management, and structuring digital workflows.
