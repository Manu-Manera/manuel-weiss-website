# 1. Getting started

## Signing in

Open ``knaufdev.tempus-resource.com`` in your browser. Knauf uses **single
sign-on (SSO)**, so you will be redirected to the corporate identity
provider — sign in with your Knauf account exactly as you would for any
other internal tool. If you accidentally land on the local Tempus login
screen, just click **Continue with SSO** and you will be forwarded
correctly. As soon as the authentication completes, Tempus Resource
opens on your personal homescreen.

## The homescreen

The homescreen is your starting point every time you launch Tempus. As a
Project or Service Manager you see exactly **two tiles**:

![PM/SM Homescreen showing two tiles: Project Management and Report Management|h=260](screenshots/01_homescreen_pmsm.png)

The **Project Management** tile takes you to the grid that lists all
projects and services you are allowed to plan; the number in the bottom
left of the tile (here: *37*) shows how many records are currently in
your scope. The **Report Management** tile opens the report library
where you can run the standard PMO and finance reports built for Knauf.
Other roles — for example Resource Managers — see additional tiles
such as *Bulk Project Allocation Flatgrid* here, but PMs and SMs work
exclusively from these two entry points.

## The top bar

Independent of the page you are on, the dark blue bar at the very top
of the screen carries the global controls. From left to right, the
right-hand side of the top bar exposes a global :icon[icon_topbar_search]
search to jump to any project by name, a :icon[icon_topbar_bell] bell
that lights up red when you have unread system notifications (resource
requests, approvals, comments), a :icon[icon_topbar_menu] hamburger
menu with module shortcuts, and a :icon[icon_topbar_help] question mark
that opens the ProSymmetry Help Center in a new tab. The avatar pill on
the far right (here showing ``PM/SM (Dummy)``) drops down to your
**Profile**, **Subscriptions** and **Logout** options. These controls
do not change between modules, so once you know them you can navigate
the whole product without losing your bearings.

## What to do on day one

Before you open a project for the first time, spend two minutes on the
homescreen and confirm three things: that your tile count matches the
number of projects you actually own, that the user badge shows your own
name (not a colleague's), and that the bell shows no unread alerts —
otherwise you may be working on stale information. Once that is in
place, click the **Project Management** tile and continue with chapter
2.
